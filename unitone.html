Improving Productivity with IT
1.1. Describing the purpose of IT in modern business
One of the many purposes of Information Technology is the easy retrieval of information
also the accuracy and consistency of said information, In business Information
Technology's purpose is to help make the right choices by giving people mass amount of
information at their fingertips. Information Technology can help with customer feedback
and have an instant connection with what needs to change or be kept. Data storage has
been a big part of renovating business and of most recent Cloud storage which means the
manager can manage or monitor their resources from anywhere in the world by using a PC
or laptop, most tech companies use these cloud based systems to manage the workplace
worldwide. [1]
1.2. Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects
successfully.
To be successful in a project what is needed is to set clear goals and assign people to
certain tasks, give objectives and requirements for the project, objectives are a key
factor to a project's success adding clarity and direction and keeps the project scope
from not getting too out of control. Setting milestones within your project plan can
value the project as it gives a clear vision for upcoming deadlines. Being aware of
risks is a must and a risk assessment enables the calculation of potential risks and
can minimize setbacks as you are able to respond and address issues as they come. [2]
1.3. Plan and carry out tasks using IT – e.g. making your website.
Planning for a task is a long process which includes ideas and themes of the idea,
brainstorming how to implement the idea and the scale of idea, skills needed and skills
I could use to improve and which parts I will cover, working up a plan by gathering
information and pictures and other resources, give yourself deadlines to meet and fix
any mistakes made, product a working website and include proof of planning. [3]
1.4. Describe the risks that might impact digital projects.
There are many risks that might impact a project these include, not enough support for
the project, disengagement of the project, conflict within the team which can take form
in combative natures and disagreements on the direction of the project, uncontrollable
growth of the project, inaccurate cost estimates, lack of management meaning the manager
has no interest or too busy with other projects, changes made are of low quality,
misunderstanding between the team and unachievable expectations, resources or training
not fully gained, flexibility in design is not up to par and many others. [4]
1.5. Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software.
I would first see the strengths and weaknesses of software available then I would list
the software into must-have and nice-to-have features in order of priority, then
establish a shortlist of software suitable and mesh well with the job at hand, next
ask for demos or download trial versions of software to be 100% that its what you need
for your project and that the teams are ok with said software. [5]
1.6. Describe and provide analysis on how your chosen technologies have helped you
achieve your outcomes.
Trello was my chosen technology for project management as it layouts a clear and easy
plan to follow, github is also good for project storage for future viewing or call
backs, when it comes to trello a “kanban” system is perfect for management as it
gives you a need-to-do, doing, and done, so anyone looking at it can see what needs
to be done and what others are working on, and for github the major perk is its free
to use and great for code storage also can store images.
1.7. Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How
does GDPR impact digital projects?
The General Data Protection Regulation primary aim is to give people control over
their information, A data protection impact assessment helps identify and minimise
data protection risks of projects. With GDPR puts the customer in the driver’s seat
and the task of complying with regulations fall upon the businesses, non-complaints
are fined 4% of global revenue. General Data Protection has changed a lot of things
for companies, for example the sales teams prospects or the way marketing activities
are managed, changes in application forms had to be made for double opt-in rules
meaning they will have to fill in a form or tick a box to confirm their actions. [6]
